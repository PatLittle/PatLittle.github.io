## My Usecase

I was trying to demonstrate for a Web Team in another department, how they could build a dynamic webpage that leverages data they publish on [Open.Canada.ca](https://open.canada.ca/data) on their departmental website.<br>

I often see GC web teams manually managing the same content in multiple places, creating headaches for ongoing maintainance, and conflicting information on different pages.<br> 

Managing data & content once, and reusing it in relevant places across the enterprise web presence seems obvious to me, but currently I think that approach is being under used.
  - just from my observations, no observation of a representitive sample here.


Vibe coding up an example, instead of struggling to describe "the art of the possible" in an email, is like the 1000-word-picture on steriods ğŸ’ª<br>

#### But I didn't have a great way to share

  * Especially for a colleage more on the business side, than a web developer
  * And I wasn't looking to setup something like a GitHub page, more appropriate for ongoing use, not a one time use situation.

  Using the [GistHost](https://gisthost.github.io) tool from Simon, I was able to just push my single HTML file to a Gist on GitHub.<br>
  Then at `GistHost.io` input the Gist ID to get the link to it as a working prototype.
